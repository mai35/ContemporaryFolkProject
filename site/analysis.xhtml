Below is our analysis write up. It includes some of the results we found from our data. Since we used such a small sample there is no real way to make definite conclusions. With that said, we did find some trends that we'd like to discuss. Even if our sample size does not show great evidence it is sill possible that it could lead to further investigations.

You can see our break down of songs by theme. We have displayed them by language as well becuase that was the purpose of our analysis. You can reach that page by clicking here or on the link near the top of this page.Because of the limited size of the data we could not produce any significant results but there seems to be a correlation between politically themed songs in English and Spanish. In almost all of the cases for political, political social, and political regional, the only songs with these themes were English and Spanish (with the exception of political social which had just 2 Italian songs and 1 Japanese song). It is entirely possibly that this was skewed by the songs that we chose. Since we did not scientifically pick our songs there is bound to be some bias when it come to chosing songs. Or simply there is the fact that since both the Spanish and English song choices came from all different countries that it was more likely that there'd be more political songs available. Most countries have artists that produce at least one song that has political conotations. Therefore since the Spanish and English songs were chosen from multiple countries while the Japanese and Italian songs were from one single contry each that this affected our data. It possible that it would have been a better option to choose songs from one country and done it by country instead of by language. Languages like Spanish and English that are not singular to one country and one culture would definitely different than languages that are related to one culture and country. It is also possible that the song preferrences of the person that chose the songs affected the output of this table. Human bias is a huge issue that can affect any part of the results that were found.

We also looked at subthemes but chose to use a table instead of a bar chart like with the overall song themes. You can reach that page by clicking here or by clicking the link near the top of this page.This table shows a number of things. Again these results are not conclusive of anything because we did not have enough data to analyze. However one interesting and probably obvious to most people is that when there were a lot of culturally themed nouns in a song the song seems to most likely be a socially themed song. Another idea that this table shows about these songs is that politically themed songs have a lot of instances of war themed nouns or just politically themed nouns. Religiously themed nouns seem to occur in all different kinds of themes. Of the three basic concepts politcal, social, and regional, a religiously themed noun seems to occur in each of those themed songs. Nouns that had a love theme seemed to occur more in politically social themed songs than in just social songs. This is an interesting idea and it would be interesting to see if this was a fact or if this result was affected by the song choices. This is even less telling of trends than the bar chart because not all of the songs had nouns that had subthemes even though they had overall themes as songs themselves.

Since this is such a small data set there is plenty of further work that could be done. It is unethically to make conclusions based on the results found here and this is really only a stepping stone for further research.
